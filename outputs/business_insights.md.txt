## Calculated Fields

### 1. Profit Margin

Logic: Profit divided by Sales.

Purpose: Shows how much profit is earned from every sale. It helps compare how profitable different regions, categories, or products are.

### 2. Cost

Logic: Sales minus Profit.

Purpose: Shows the estimated cost of the products sold. It helps compare costs across different products and regions.

### 3. Average Order Value (AOV)

Logic: Total Sales divided by the total number of unique orders.

Purpose: Shows the average amount customers spend on each order. It helps identify which customers or sales channels generate higher-value orders.

### 4. Return Rate

Logic: Number of returned orders divided by the total number of orders.

Purpose: Shows the percentage of orders that were returned. It helps find products or regions with more returns.

### 5. Shipping Delay Bucket

Logic: Groups delivery days into ranges such as 0–2 Days, 3–5 Days, 6–7 Days, and More than 7 Days.

Purpose: Groups orders based on delivery time. It helps compare sales, returns, and customer ratings for different delivery speeds.
